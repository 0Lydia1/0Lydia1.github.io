Stefan Zweig

Austrian novelist, playwright, journalist and biographer.
At the height of his literary career, in the 1920s and 1930s, he was one
of the most widely translated and most popular writers in the world

Here's a time line of Stefan Zweig's life:

  • 1881 - Born in Vienna
  • 1904 - Earned a doctoral degree with a thesis on "The Philosophy of Hippolyte Taine".
  • 1910 - Visit to India
  • 1912 - Visit to North America
  • 1913 - He settles in Salzburg, where he would live for almost twenty years
  • 1920 - Marries Friderike Maria von Winternitz
  • 1934 - As a result of the Nazi Party's rise in Germany, Zweig emigrated to England
  • 1936 - His books were banned in Germany by the Nazi regime
  • 1938 - Divorced Friderike Maria von Winternitz
  • 1939 - New marriage with Charlotte Elisabeth Altmann
  • 1940 - Moved briefly to New York and then to Brazil, where he settled. He would declare himself in love with the country, writing about it in the book Brazil, Land of the Future.
  • 1942 - Desperate for the future of Europe and its culture, because they believed that Nazism would spread to the entire planet, on February 22, he and his wife committed suicide
